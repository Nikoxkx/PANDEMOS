Pandemics don't wait. Neither should awareness.

**PANDEMOS** is an open-source disease surveillance and public health education platform built for researchers, students, educators, and informed citizens who refuse to be caught off guard. It pulls from the world's most trusted health institutions and presents that intelligence with precision, transparency, and zero noise тАФ because in a public health crisis, clarity is infrastructure.

## ЁЯзн What PANDEMOS Is

PANDEMOS sits at the intersection of **surveillance intelligence**, **public health literacy**, and **open data**.

It is not a news aggregator.
It is not a panic machine.
It is not another dashboard that goes dark the moment funding dries up.

It is a **structured, sourced, and sober lens** on the state of infectious disease тАФ globally, historically, and right now.

## ЁЯУб Data Sources

All data is sourced exclusively from verified, official public health institutions and peer-reviewed literature. No exceptions.

<br />

| Institution | Role |
|:---|:---|
| **WHO** | World Health Organization тАФ global outbreak declarations and guidance |
| **CDC** | U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention тАФ domestic and global surveillance |
| **ECDC** | European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control тАФ European outbreak tracking |
| **Africa CDC** | Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention тАФ continental surveillance |
| **PAHO** | Pan American Health Organization тАФ Americas regional data |
| **The Lancet / NEJM / Nature** | Peer-reviewed primary literature |
| **GISAID / Nextstrain** | Genomic sequencing and phylogenetic surveillance |
| **Our World in Data** | Aggregated, open-access epidemiological datasets |
| **MSF / UNICEF / GAVI** | Field-level humanitarian health intelligence |

<br />

> **PANDEMOS does not manufacture, estimate, or editorialize health data.**
> If a source says it, we cite it. If we can't cite it, it doesn't appear.
